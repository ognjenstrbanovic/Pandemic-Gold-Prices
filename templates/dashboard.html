Overview


In times of uncertainty the public strive to find the silver lining, however in our project in this time of Covid19 and economic uncertainty we have endeavored as it were to find the golden lining.

In the broader context of history because nothing happens in a vaccum, the main take aways from our investigation is that of out of the five pandemics, (that have occurred in the last 100 years) the first three pandemics the price of gold wre not affected. Based on our research it appears that the price of gold was more affected by monetary policy and the industrial output than the pandemics themselves, as it will become evident with the charts and graph that we have created.
However for the last two pandemics this is not true, in fact, in a new service economy that is served by modern monetary policy, the price of gold has been subject to market fluctuations and to some degree by the pandemics in the last 20 years. For example in 2009 the price of gold grew by 27.86% in that year alone and this year during the Coronavirus pandemic the price of gold is up 24%. It can be argued that in 2009 the price of gold was much more affected by the financial meltdown than by the pandemic, but one thing we have learned is that major pandemics happens during extreme moments in history that are engolfed by either wars or financial crisis.
