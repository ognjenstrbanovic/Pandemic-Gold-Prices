DJIA Prices/Gold Prices Ratio During the 1918 Pandemic (H1N1 Virus)







We looked at this in a 5-year period: from 1917 to 1922. We wanted to capture the before, during, and after-effects of the so-called "Spanish Flu", which was most prevalent during 1918-19. If you look at the chart above, you can see that it is volatile-looking, much like a stock, for instance. As one can see from the chart below, the peak of H1N1 deaths was during the winter of 1918-19. If you look above, the biggest rush to gold was during that time. However, the fact is that there were many confounding variables that had an impact on both sets of prices. For example, during this time, WWI was waged, climaxed, and ended. The Russian Revolution was ongoing. The list goes on. But, reverting back to what we set out to find, which was whether gold is a hedge during pandemics such as this one, we just cannot say with certainty (with so much gray area) that people were running to buy gold over stocks at the height of the pandemic, and whether it is solely because of this that they bought gold.
